Introduction

I am a CIFRE PhD student working with the LIPADE lab at Paris Descartes University and the Noah's Ark Lab at Huawei Technologies France, co-advised by Ievgen Redko, Themis Palpanas and Hassine Moungla. My work delves deeply into the realm of time series analysis, with a particular emphasis on classification and forecasting tasks. In the initial phase of my doctoral research, I delved into methods for data augmentation and the synthetic generation of time series to address the challenges of extensive labeling and data cleaning. Progressing further, my focus shifted towards the exploration of adversarial attacks, where I developed cutting-edge attack strategies alongside robust defense mechanisms. Subsequently, my attention turned to forecasting in time series, particularly investigating the limitations of transformers in this context and strategizing on enhancing their performance. Currently, my research is centered on leveraging the capabilities of Large Language Models for forecasting in time series analysis. My work, while centered on time series classification and forecasting, can be readily adapted to other fields like computer vision or NLP. Previously, I earned an engineering diploma in Data Science, Statistics and Learning from ENSAE and a Machine Learning Research Master degree from Ecole Polytechnique.
